WINNING EMAIL TEMPLATE — "your support line after hours"
=========================================================
This is the well-performing template the whole project scales. It's an
AI-voice-agent pitch (Tanay → e-commerce shops) built around ONE specific,
verifiable gap: the store's phone support is voicemail-only / weekday-only,
so after-hours buyer questions go unanswered.

The skeleton below stays fixed. Only the bracketed parts flex per store, and
ONLY when the site actually states them. If the gap can't be verified on the
live site, the store is skipped — never invent the gap.

  FIXED   — the offer, the structure, the sign-off, the CTA
  FLEX    — [phone], [the specific hours/days gap], cited from the store's site


----------------------------------- TEMPLATE -----------------------------------

To:      [store support email — found on the live site]
Subject: your support line after hours

Hey team,

I saw your support line, [PHONE], is [THE VERIFIED GAP — e.g. "voicemail-only
and your team's available Monday through Friday"] - so a weekend message
doesn't get answered until the following [Monday or Tuesday]. For an e-commerce
store that's often exactly when buyers have "where's my order?" questions and
are deciding whether to trust you with a purchase.

I specialize in setting up AI voice agents that answer the phone 24/7 and
handle those questions - order status, returns, your common FAQs - fully
trained on your brand voice and your own docs.

Worth a quick call to see if it'd be a fit?

Thanks,
Tanay


------------------------------ EXAMPLE (P.F. Candle Co.) ------------------------
The template above IS the P.F. Candle Co. email — it was a verbatim match
because their contact page literally says the line is voicemail-only, Mon–Fri,
with weekend messages answered the following Mon/Tue. For the other stores the
offer stayed identical and only the gap hook changed to what each site
actually states (e.g. "only staffed Monday–Friday, 8:30am–4pm").
